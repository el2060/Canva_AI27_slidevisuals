Why a dedicated staff space for Digital, AI & EdTech?

"I'm quite busy — I'll usually pick things up online or via Teams
and try it out on my own."

"Then I'll see how to apply it to my module or work
— materials, content, day-to-day tasks."

"But beyond what's already available, I'm not always sure what tools to use
— trying new ones usually means putting up requests or getting access, so I tend to stick with what I have."

  • Join sessions or access resources to get oriented and pick up ideas when I can
  • Apply EdTech / AI tools within my own module / work, focused on immediate needs
  • Mostly individual, self-directed use
  • Progress depends on time and what I can figure out on my own

What might the space look like in terms of layout?

Building on the existing Studio27 space

How the space will be set up

Zone 01
Gallery
(Discover)

Interactive stations showcasing AI, Digital & EdTech tools, features and use cases

Zone 02
Tech Bench
(Try)

Workstations with pre-set tools and sandbox setups for hands-on exploration

Zone 03
Open Pit
(Experiment)

Flexible space for demos, guided experimentation and mini challenges

Zone 04
Training Room
(Learn)

Workshops, clinics and facilitated sessions (onsite / hybrid)

Zone 05
Collaboration Bench
(Co-create)

Small-group space for discussions, consultations and workflow design

Each zone is designed to support a different mode of engagement — from browsing and discovery through to hands-on work and collaborative problem-solving.

Staff can move between zones based on their needs, or spend focused time in one area.

Driving awareness and sustained use

Usage will be driven through a mix of structured sessions (e.g. workshops, show & tell, mini challenges/build sessions) and on-demand support (1–1 / small group). Staff engage around real tasks — with opportunities to try, get help, and learn from others in the same space.
